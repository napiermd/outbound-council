# Market Access and Health Economics Playbook

Use this playbook when a council output must survive hospital value analysis, payer/economic scrutiny, budget review, or adoption economics.

## When to activate

- A hospital asks for economic justification.
- A value-analysis packet is being prepared.
- A pilot must prove commercially meaningful outcomes.
- IntuBlade makes claims about cost, resource use, infection prevention, training, standardization, or software/AI value.
- The buyer asks whether the product is reimbursed, coded, covered, budget-neutral, or worth standardizing.

## Core framework

1. **Comparator**: What is the current state being replaced or supplemented?
2. **Decision owner**: Who cares financially, operationally, clinically, and administratively?
3. **Economic mechanism**: Does value come from avoided capital, reduced reprocessing, standardization, fewer delays, training efficiency, risk reduction, documentation, or utilization?
4. **Evidence type**: Is support clinical trial, real-world evidence, budget-impact model, cost comparison, value dossier, pilot data, or expert consensus?
5. **Adoption gate**: Is the next decision a trial, VAC review, contract, stocking, protocol update, or scale decision?

## Coding, coverage, and payment

- Do not imply new reimbursement unless a source supports it.
- For a single-use video laryngoscope, the default economic case is usually hospital/agency purchasing, budget impact, risk reduction, training, and workflow value rather than procedure-specific reimbursement.
- If software, QA/QI, or procedural guidance becomes billable or payer-relevant, create a separate reimbursement path review before making claims.
- Questions to answer:
  - Is there an existing purchasing or billing pathway?
  - Does the buyer need reimbursement, or only value-analysis approval?
  - Who pays for the device, disposables, software, training, and support?
  - What coding/coverage/payment claims are explicitly off-limits?

## Budget-impact model inputs

- Current equipment model: reusable VL, direct laryngoscopy, disposable blades, mixed cart, or no video coverage.
- Current costs: capital, blades, batteries, reprocessing, repair, downtime, stocking, lost equipment, training, and waste.
- Event costs to model only when evidence supports them: difficult intubation, prolonged length of stay, contamination/reprocessing failures, failed attempts, training remediation, documentation gaps.
- Pilot measures:
  - utilization by setting
  - first-attempt success where collectable
  - failed/aborted attempts
  - training completion
  - device availability
  - stocking reliability
  - user confidence
  - review/debrief completion

## Value dossier outline

- Executive summary: problem, comparator, proposed use, decision requested.
- Clinical need: airway-readiness gap and affected stakeholders.
- Product fit: where IntuBlade is primary, backup, training, or review tool.
- Evidence: claims, limits, and source links.
- Economic case: cost model, assumptions, pilot metrics, sensitivity points.
- Operational case: stocking, training, disposal, support, failure modes.
- Risk case: infection prevention, patient safety, workflow, governance.
- Pilot proposal: owners, timeline, success criteria, decision date.

## Draft review criteria

- Names the comparator.
- Separates clinical value from economic value.
- States assumptions clearly.
- Avoids reimbursement overclaiming.
- Includes pilot metrics that a hospital can actually collect.
- Defines the next adoption gate.

## Common rewrite moves

- Replace "saves money" with "may reduce capital/reprocessing/training/availability burden; pilot should measure X, Y, Z."
- Replace "better outcomes" with "supports a measurable airway quality program; outcomes claim requires evidence."
- Replace "single-use is safer" with "single-use removes reprocessing steps and may reduce contamination workflow risk; do not claim lower infection rates without evidence."
- Replace "AI adds value" with "software value must be measured through review compliance, training remediation, documentation quality, or workflow lift."
