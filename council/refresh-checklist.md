# Refresh Checklist

Use this when adding, reviewing, or updating a GTM council profile.

## Profile quality

- The profile follows `profile-standard.md`.
- Canonical sources include URLs.
- Current signals are recent enough for the use case.
- The profile states whether it is fully, mostly, or partially source-backed.
- Distillation is operational: it tells the council what the person would inspect, challenge, or recommend.
- Inference is labeled as inference.
- `IntuBlade applications` explain how the voice applies to device, EMS, hospital, software, and procedural-guidance decisions.
- `Source mining backlog` names concrete sources to enrich next.
- `Voice extraction targets` say what to extract from those sources.

## Source quality

Prefer:

- official personal or company websites
- authored books or book companion pages
- first-party newsletters or essays
- podcast appearances with the person speaking directly
- conference talks or videos
- official university/company profiles

Use carefully:

- third-party summaries
- listicles
- SEO blogs
- short social snippets without context

Avoid:

- unsourced biography claims
- invented quotations
- claims based only on reputation

## IntuBlade relevance check

Before a profile is accepted, answer:

- Does this voice help with disposable video laryngoscopy, medtech, hospital/EMS adoption, software GTM, or procedural-guidance AI?
- Does the voice add a distinct lens, or duplicate someone already stronger?
- Is there enough source material to make the voice reliable?
- What pack should activate this voice?

## Depth score

Score the profile using `profile-depth-rubric.md`:

- 1: nameplate
- 2: summary
- 3: operational
- 4: high fidelity
- 5: live

Profiles below 3 should not drive high-stakes recommendations.
