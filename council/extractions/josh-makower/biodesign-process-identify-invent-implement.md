## Why this source matters

This is the core public description of the Stanford Biodesign process that Makower helped architect. It gives the council a repeatable structure for judging whether IntuBlade's GTM and roadmap are grounded in an important unmet need rather than in a product-first story.

## Frameworks

- Source-backed: Stanford Biodesign frames innovation as a learnable process, not creative magic.
- Source-backed: The process has three phases: identify, invent, implement.
- Source-backed: The identify phase starts with direct observation across the care cycle, collecting many needs before filtering.
- Source-backed: The invent phase compares many concepts against criteria for satisfying the need, uses rapid prototypes, and filters against IP, business model, reimbursement, and regulatory pathways.
- Source-backed: The implement phase includes prototyping/testing, patenting, regulatory approval, reimbursement, market potential, and funding.
- IntuBlade inference: Do not start with "single-use video laryngoscope" as the need. Start with observed airway problems: availability, contamination risk, training variability, poor first-pass visibility, review gaps, or data-poor QA.

## Language patterns

- Source-backed: Need finding comes before solutioning.
- Source-backed: Stanford uses disciplined language around unmet health needs, direct observation, stakeholder effects, rigorous filtering, and execution planning.
- IntuBlade inference: Strong IntuBlade language should sound like "observed airway readiness gap" before it sounds like "new device feature."

## Stories and examples

- Source-backed: The process asks trainees to gather a large body of needs through observation before prioritizing the few with major impact potential.
- Source-backed: Invention is framed as survival of the fittest among many concepts, not protection of the first idea.
- IntuBlade inference: An EMS campaign should be able to say what airway events, crews, training contexts, and equipment constraints were observed before asserting that disposable video airway coverage is the right answer.

## Objections and warnings

- Source-backed: The process warns against treating innovation as unstructured inspiration.
- Source-backed: It also warns indirectly against late-stage discovery of reimbursement, regulatory, business model, or implementation barriers.
- IntuBlade inference: Makower would challenge a GTM plan that sells the blade before proving the need intensity and implementation path by segment.
