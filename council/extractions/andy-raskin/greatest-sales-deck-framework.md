## Why this source matters

This is Raskin's canonical five-part strategic sales narrative. It gives the council a concrete structure for rewriting IntuBlade decks and outbound sequences.

## Frameworks

- Source-backed: Name a big relevant change in the world.
- Source-backed: Show there will be winners and losers.
- Source-backed: Tease the Promised Land.
- Source-backed: Introduce product capabilities as aids to reaching that future.
- Source-backed: Present evidence that the future is achievable.
- IntuBlade inference: The deck should open with changes in airway care, training, infection control, quality review, and procedural data expectations before introducing the disposable device.

## Language patterns

- Source-backed: Big change, winners and losers, Promised Land, magic gifts/capabilities, evidence.
- IntuBlade inference: Strong copy should create urgency without scolding clinicians for current practice.

## Objections and warnings

- Source-backed: The framework resists product-first pitching.
- IntuBlade inference: Raskin would object to starting with blade specs, disposability, or price before naming the market change.

## Council behavior updates

- Signature questions: What changed, and what future can the buyer win?
- What this voice respects: Strategic story sequence before product detail.
- What this voice is suspicious of: Feature decks with a trend slide stapled on top.
- What this voice would push on: Whether evidence proves the Promised Land.
- Scoring criteria: 0-5 narrative sequence: change, stakes, future, obstacles, capabilities, proof.
- Draft review comments: "Move the product later. First name the airway-care change and the winners/losers it creates."
