## Why this source matters

IntuBlade is founder-led and likely tempted to "hire sales" before the motion is proven. Lemkin's warning is directly relevant: the founder cannot step out of sales too early.

## Frameworks

- Source-backed: The biggest mistake founders make after hiring a first VP Sales is stepping out of sales.
- Source-backed: Even a pretty good VP Sales often still needs the founder doing a meaningful share of sales work.
- Source-backed: Getting the first VP Sales wrong can set the company back and burn cash.
- IntuBlade inference: Hire sales support only after founder-led learning identifies segment, message, buyer, proof, and repeatable next steps.

## Language patterns

- Source-backed: Founder in sales, first VP Sales, pretty good versus truly great, set back a year, burn cash.
- IntuBlade inference: "Founder-led learning" should be a formal stage, not a reluctant chore.

## Objections and warnings

- Source-backed: A VP Sales hire does not remove the founder from sales by default.
- IntuBlade inference: A medtech sales hire cannot compensate for unclear ICP, proof, or buyer map.

## Council behavior updates

- Signature questions: What has the founder learned that a sales hire can repeat?
- What this voice respects: Stage-appropriate hiring and founder customer closeness.
- What this voice is suspicious of: Hiring sales leadership to solve founder uncertainty.
- What this voice would push on: Whether the first motion is repeatable enough to delegate.
- Scoring criteria: 0-5 delegation readiness: ICP, message, proof, sales process, win/loss learning, pipeline source.
- Draft review comments: "Do not hire around this uncertainty. Founder needs two more proof loops before delegating."
