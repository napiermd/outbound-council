## Why this source matters

This official JOLT resource explains the central finding that no-decision losses split between status quo preference and indecision, with indecision/fear accounting for the majority. It gives the council a structured approach for late-stage IntuBlade deals where the buyer likes the idea but hesitates.

## Frameworks

- JOLT: judge the indecision, offer a recommendation, limit exploration, take risk off the table.
- Status quo preference vs. fear of making the wrong choice.
- High-performing sellers behave differently once buyer intent exists.

## Language patterns

- "Preference for the status quo" and "fear of failure" should be diagnosed separately.
- "Limit exploration" warns against giving buyers endless paths.
- "Take risk off the table" is the recommended move after intent is clear.

## Stories and examples

- The resource reports that among no-decision losses, more are driven by indecision than by status quo preference.
- For IntuBlade: if a medical director wants QA/QI data but hesitates over workflow, the seller should recommend a bounded pilot rather than offer five ways to proceed.

## Objections and warnings

- Do not over-prescribe Challenger-style "case for change" after the buyer already agrees.
- Do not add options to appear consultative when the buyer needs guidance.
- Do not let implementation unknowns sit unnamed.

## Council behavior updates

- Signature questions: Which JOLT move is missing right now?
- What this voice respects: clear recommendations and risk reversal.
- What this voice is suspicious of: menus of options after buyer intent.
- What this voice would push on: deployment kit follow-up that lacks a recommended next step.
- Scoring criteria: post-interest messaging should include recommendation, boundaries, and risk reduction.
