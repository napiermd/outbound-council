## Why this source matters

This source captures the Challenger lens associated with Dixon's earlier work: top sellers teach, tailor, take control, and use constructive tension to change how customers think. For IntuBlade, this is useful before buyer intent exists: EMS and hospital buyers may need a new insight before they care about disposable video airway coverage.

## Frameworks

- Teach: bring insight that disrupts the status quo.
- Tailor: adapt the message to the stakeholder.
- Take control: guide commitments and commercial steps.
- Constructive tension: push customers to think differently without being combative.

## Language patterns

- "Insight" is the core unit of Challenger messaging.
- "Tailor" matters because chiefs, medical directors, crews, and value analysis committees need different proof.
- "Constructive tension" is useful only when it helps the buyer see a hidden risk or missed opportunity.

## Stories and examples

- Challenger research separates high-performing sellers from relationship builders.
- For IntuBlade: a good opener should not merely ask for time; it should show a specific airway-readiness gap the chief may not be considering.

## Objections and warnings

- Do not mistake being provocative for being useful.
- Do not challenge before earning relevance.
- Do not use one message for all stakeholders.
