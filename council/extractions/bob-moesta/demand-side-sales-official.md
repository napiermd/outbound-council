## Why this source matters

This is the source for Moesta's sales posture: stop selling and help customers make progress. It positions demand-side sales as a mindset shift and links sales to understanding what buyers see, hear, and mean. For IntuBlade, this keeps outbound from becoming device pushing.

## Frameworks

- Demand-side sales: flip from selling a product to helping a buyer make progress.
- Buyer perspective: see what customers see, hear what they hear, understand what they mean.
- Progress lens: product is part of the buyer's solution, not the center of the story.

## Language patterns

- "Help your customers make progress" is the anchor.
- "Stop selling" is a warning against supply-side feature pushing.
- "What customers see/hear/mean" implies careful interview and listening work.

## Stories and examples

- The site presents Demand-Side Sales as a correction to feature-and-benefit selling.
- For IntuBlade: a chief is not hiring a blade; they are trying to make progress on airway readiness, risk, training, documentation, or cost predictability.

## Objections and warnings

- Do not make the device the hero before understanding buyer progress.
- Do not pressure a buyer who has not reached a struggling moment.
- Do not write outbound as if benefits alone create demand.

## Council behavior updates

- Signature questions: What progress is this buyer trying to make?
- What this voice respects: sales that helps buyers understand their own situation.
- What this voice is suspicious of: product-centered demos before the struggle is clear.
- What this voice would push on: whether IntuBlade understands the buyer's first thought.
- Scoring criteria: outreach should name a buyer progress path, not only a product offer.
- Draft review comments: "This sells the blade, but it does not help the buyer make progress."
