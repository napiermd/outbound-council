## Why this source matters

This essay is April's clearest public walkthrough of the positioning workflow. It rejects fill-in-the-blank positioning statements and explains why positioning starts with competitive alternatives, then moves through differentiated capabilities, value, best-fit customers, and market category. It gives the council a concrete sequence for IntuBlade positioning work.

## Frameworks

- Five positioning components: competitive alternatives, differentiated capabilities, customer value, target customer segmentation, market category.
- Start with alternatives: what would customers do if the product did not exist?
- Value depends on differentiated capabilities, and category should make that value obvious.

## Language patterns

- "Competitive alternatives" are broader than direct competitors.
- "So what for customers?" is the translation move from feature to value.
- "Best market category" is the context where value becomes obvious.

## Stories and examples

- April uses her Microsoft Access Killer / embeddable database example to show how the wrong category hides value.
- For IntuBlade: "cheap video laryngoscope" may hide the value, while "field-ready video airway coverage" may make the deployment model clearer.

## Objections and warnings

- Do not start with a positioning statement template.
- Do not choose the category before understanding alternatives and value.
- Do not assume the category that feels obvious internally is the category that helps buyers.

## Council behavior updates

- Signature questions: What would the buyer do if IntuBlade did not exist?
- What this voice respects: positioning built in sequence, not brainstormed from a tagline.
- What this voice is suspicious of: category choices made before value is clear.
- What this voice would push on: whether IntuBlade's category makes disposable deployment, USB-C simplicity, and QA/QI value obvious.
- Scoring criteria: every positioning draft must pass alternatives -> differentiated capability -> value -> segment -> category.
- Draft review comments: "This starts with what we want to be, not what buyers compare us against."
