# Enrichment Protocol

This protocol turns a profile from a useful summary into an operational voice.

The goal is not more biography. The goal is higher-fidelity decision behavior.

## Enrichment levels

### Level 1: Identified

The voice has a name, domain, and reason to exist in the council.

Use only for brainstorming. Do not let this voice drive a recommendation.

### Level 2: Source-backed

The voice has canonical sources, current signals, and a documented decision lens.

Use for normal council activations.

### Level 3: Operational

The voice has:

- source-backed frameworks
- reusable signature questions
- examples of how they reason through tradeoffs
- source-mined language patterns
- IntuBlade-specific applications
- known tensions with other voices

Use for strategy, scoring, campaign rewrites, and deal inspection.

### Level 4: Live

The voice has a recent-signal file and source refresh within the last 90 days.

Use for high-stakes decisions, current-market claims, or public-facing strategy.

## What to enrich

Every strong voice should eventually have six layers:

1. **Canonical worldview**: what this person seems to believe about markets, buyers, sales, products, or healthcare.
2. **Framework library**: named or repeatable frameworks they use.
3. **Story bank**: examples, cases, anecdotes, before/after patterns, and cautionary tales.
4. **Language bank**: recurring phrases, distinctions, warnings, and question styles.
5. **Decision heuristics**: how they would choose between competing paths.
6. **IntuBlade translation**: what their lens means for disposable VL, EMS, hospitals, QA/QI, software, and procedural guidance.

## Source mining workflow

For each voice:

1. Start with the profile's `Canonical sources`.
2. Pull 3-5 high-density sources:
   - one book or long-form essay
   - one podcast or talk
   - one recent signal
   - one source that shows how they critique bad strategy
   - one source that shows them applying the framework to a real company or buyer
3. Extract:
   - frameworks
   - signature questions
   - terms of art
   - repeated warnings
   - examples and stories
   - what they would say "no" to
4. Convert extracts into council behavior:
   - scoring criteria
   - draft review comments
   - deal inspection questions
   - outbound rewrites
   - strategy recommendations
5. Mark every direct quote with a source URL. Prefer paraphrase unless the exact language is load-bearing.

## Do not overfit the voice

Avoid:

- fake quotes
- parody cadence
- exaggerated personality
- overconfident current claims from old sources
- treating one podcast comment as doctrine
- importing advice outside the person's domain

## IntuBlade translation checklist

For every enriched profile, answer:

- How would this voice frame a single-use video laryngoscope?
- How would this voice explain why EMS/fire should change now?
- How would this voice handle hospital adoption and value analysis?
- How would this voice think about QA/QI software and procedural guidance?
- What would this voice warn Andrew not to do?
- What kind of outbound would this voice reject?
