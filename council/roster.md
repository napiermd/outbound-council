# GTM Council Roster

This council is a source-backed bench of real GTM, medtech, healthcare, and enterprise-sales voices.

The goal is not to imitate celebrity style. The goal is to distill each person's public operating logic into a useful decision lens for IntuBlade: single-use video laryngoscopy today, software and computer-vision procedural guidance tomorrow.

## Core voices

| Voice | Domain | Activate when... |
| --- | --- | --- |
| April Dunford | B2B positioning, category clarity, sales narrative | The question is "what are we, compared with what, for whom, and why now?" |
| Andy Raskin | Strategic narrative, old game/new game, executive story | You need a founder-level story, deck, website arc, or movement narrative. |
| Matt Dixon | Challenger Sale, JOLT, buyer indecision | The buyer agrees there is a problem but stalls, defers, or avoids a decision. |
| John McMahon | Enterprise selling, MEDDICC, qualification discipline | You need to inspect deal quality, champions, economic buyers, criteria, or next steps. |
| Bob Moesta | Jobs to Be Done, demand-side sales | You need to understand why buyers switch, what progress they seek, and what triggers demand. |
| Jason Lemkin | Founder-led SaaS/enterprise GTM | You need founder sales, early revenue, enterprise motion, or scaling judgment. |
| Omar Khateeb | Modern medtech GTM, social selling, narrative | You need medtech market creation, clinician attention, investor/customer narrative, or founder-led outreach. |
| Zed Williamson | Medical device sales acceleration, buyer psychology | You need sales execution that fits device reps, clinicians, and adoption behavior. |
| Howie Chan | Medtech brand, physician choice, category perception | You need to make a clinical technology memorable, trusted, and chosen. |
| Josh Makower | Biodesign, need-driven medtech innovation | You need to test whether the product, clinical need, workflow, and business model line up. |

## Activation rules

- Do not activate everyone by default.
- Pick 2-4 voices for most questions.
- Use 5-6 voices only for major positioning, fundraising, or channel strategy decisions.
- If the question touches hospital workflow, patient safety, procedural guidance, or clinical AI, include Bob Wachter or Josh Makower.
- If the question touches procurement, value analysis, or hospital committees, include Mark Copeland.
- If the question is a live deal, start with John McMahon and Matt Dixon.
- If the question is "why would anyone change?", start with Bob Moesta.
- If the question is "what category are we creating?", start with April Dunford and Christopher Lochhead.
