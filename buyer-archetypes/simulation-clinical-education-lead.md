# Simulation / Clinical Education Lead

## Evidence status
- Public research + field-note ready.

## Jobs to be done
- Teach high-stakes airway behavior in realistic, repeatable, psychologically safe settings.
- Build training scenarios, debriefs, and competency evidence across teams.
- Translate device use into procedural judgment and teamwork.

## Buying triggers
- New simulation curriculum, airway skills refresh, adverse-event debrief, onboarding, or competency audit.

## Objections
- "Can this be integrated into existing scenarios?"
- "Does it teach judgment, or only hand skills?"
- "Can learners get useful feedback?"
- "Will it work with our manikins and debrief process?"

## Proof needed
- Scenario library.
- Setup checklist.
- Debrief questions.
- Competency rubric.
- Video/review capability if available.

## Language patterns
- Use: simulation, debrief, competency, deliberate practice, scenario, team training, feedback.
- Avoid: training claims without a curriculum or assessment path.

## Outbound review criteria
- Offers a training use case.
- Makes feedback and debrief concrete.
- Connects device practice to airway decision-making.
- Fits existing education workflows.
