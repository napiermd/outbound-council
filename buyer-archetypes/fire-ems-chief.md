# Fire/EMS Chief

## Evidence status
- Public research + field-note ready.

## Jobs to be done
- Keep crews ready, protected, equipped, and defensible under public scrutiny.
- Allocate budget across operations, training, equipment, staffing, and political priorities.
- Avoid purchases that create logistics burden or low utilization.

## Buying triggers
- Budget cycle, grant window, capital replacement, new medical director priority, or high-visibility call review.
- Training gaps across stations or shifts.
- Public safety leadership push for modernization or standardization.

## Objections
- "How many units would need this?"
- "Will crews actually use it?"
- "What does deployment look like across stations?"
- "How do we justify this against other needs?"

## Proof needed
- Deployment model by unit/station.
- Training time and maintenance requirements.
- Budget range and grant/procurement fit.
- Peer agency proof or pilot structure.
- Operational risk removed by single-use readiness.

## Language patterns
- Use: readiness, standardization, deployment, crews, stations, budget cycle, low-friction rollout.
- Avoid: hospital jargon, physician-only framing, or complex technical feature lists.

## Outbound review criteria
- Opens with an operational signal.
- Makes the next step easy and low-risk.
- Shows respect for budget and crew burden.
- Includes a concrete question a chief can answer quickly.
