# Supply Chain / Procurement Lead

## Evidence status
- Public research + field-note ready.

## Jobs to be done
- Ensure products can be sourced, contracted, stocked, replenished, and governed without creating supply or compliance risk.
- Reduce vendor and SKU friction while supporting clinical needs.

## Buying triggers
- Clinician request, trial conversion, contract review, shortage, substitute search, or standardization push.

## Objections
- "Are you an approved vendor?"
- "What are the SKUs, shelf life, storage, and minimums?"
- "How stable is supply?"
- "Does this duplicate existing contracts?"

## Proof needed
- SKU and ordering information.
- Pricing and contract structure.
- Vendor onboarding requirements.
- Shelf life, packaging, storage, and stocking plan.
- Trial-to-purchase path and support contacts.

## Language patterns
- Use: SKU, stocking, vendor onboarding, contract, supply continuity, standardization, trial conversion.
- Avoid: clinical enthusiasm as a substitute for procurement readiness.

## Outbound review criteria
- Makes the buying path legible.
- Does not surprise procurement late.
- Identifies supply-chain questions before trial.
- Keeps clinical and procurement workflows connected.
