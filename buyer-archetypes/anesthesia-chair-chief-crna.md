# Anesthesia Chair / Chief CRNA

## Evidence status
- Public research + field-note ready.

## Jobs to be done
- Maintain safe, efficient, standardized airway management across rooms, locations, and provider preferences.
- Balance equipment quality, ergonomics, availability, contamination/reprocessing, and cost.
- Protect throughput and clinician confidence.

## Buying triggers
- Capital replacement, standardization initiative, ambulatory expansion, infection-control pressure, or preference-card cleanup.
- Need for backup airway access outside main OR rooms.

## Objections
- "Our current system works."
- "Will this match clinician habits?"
- "Is image quality and blade geometry acceptable?"
- "Does single-use create waste or cost concerns?"

## Proof needed
- Comparable blade geometry and user experience.
- Image quality and reliability.
- Cost comparison versus reusable/capital systems.
- Availability and stocking model.
- Use-case clarity: primary, backup, offsite, training, or emergency.

## Language patterns
- Use: familiar technique, ergonomics, availability, room turnover, offsite anesthesia, standardization, backup coverage.
- Avoid: implying anesthesia lacks skill or needs a beginner device.

## Outbound review criteria
- Respects existing expertise.
- Explains where the product fits without threatening current systems.
- Addresses economics and waste honestly.
- Uses OR/offsite workflow language.
