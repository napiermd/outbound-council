# ED Medical Director / Airway Lead

## Evidence status
- Public research + field-note ready.

## Jobs to be done
- Improve emergency airway reliability across providers, shifts, and acuity.
- Build an airway quality program with equipment, training, review, and escalation.
- Prevent bad outcomes from anatomy, physiology, contamination, and human factors.

## Buying triggers
- Airway adverse event, quality review, new airway lead role, device standardization, or resident/trainee training issue.
- Interest in first-pass success, peri-intubation hypoxia, aspiration, or documentation quality.

## Objections
- "Where does this fit in our airway cart and algorithm?"
- "Will attendings, residents, RT, and nursing all support this?"
- "What evidence applies to our ED?"
- "How do we prevent overreliance on video?"

## Proof needed
- ED workflow map.
- Airway cart/stocking plan.
- Training and credentialing path.
- QI metrics and review cadence.
- Evidence for routine and difficult airway contexts.

## Language patterns
- Use: airway program, first-pass success, peri-intubation physiology, human factors, QI review, escalation.
- Avoid: device-only claims or treating the ED like the OR.

## Outbound review criteria
- Names ED-specific airway pressure.
- Connects device adoption to an airway quality program.
- Respects multidisciplinary workflow.
- Bounds claims by patient context and evidence.
