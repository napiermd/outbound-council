# EMS Training Officer / Educator

## Evidence status
- Public research + field-note ready.

## Jobs to be done
- Build and maintain airway competence across varied experience levels.
- Convert training into reliable field behavior under stress.
- Create repeatable skills refreshes, simulations, and after-action learning.

## Buying triggers
- Skills drift, new hire onboarding, CE calendar, failed airway review, or simulation program update.
- Medical director asks for measurable airway competency.
- Need for video review or objective feedback.

## Objections
- "Will this be easy to teach?"
- "Does it reinforce or confuse existing airway algorithms?"
- "Can we use it for review without shaming crews?"
- "How much time does it take to get competent?"

## Proof needed
- Training module, checklist, and competency criteria.
- Simulation use cases.
- Video/review workflow if available.
- Troubleshooting guide for field conditions.
- Alignment with protocols and medical direction.

## Language patterns
- Use: deliberate practice, competency, refreshers, simulation, debrief, field translation.
- Avoid: "plug-and-play" if training is still required.

## Outbound review criteria
- Speaks to teaching and adoption, not only equipment.
- Offers a training artifact or pilot.
- Names how feedback will improve without increasing blame.
- Fits EMS time constraints.
