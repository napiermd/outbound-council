# Respiratory Therapy Director

## Evidence status
- Public research + field-note ready.

## Jobs to be done
- Support airway, ventilation, equipment readiness, and interdisciplinary care across ED, ICU, transport, and procedures.
- Reduce device confusion and ensure staff can assist effectively.
- Maintain education, documentation, and equipment stewardship.

## Buying triggers
- Airway cart standardization, ICU/ED airway event review, device training burden, or respiratory education refresh.
- Need for better review of airway/ventilation interface.

## Objections
- "Will RT be expected to own this?"
- "How does it fit with ventilator/capnography/suction workflows?"
- "Does it add another device to troubleshoot?"
- "Who trains and stocks it?"

## Proof needed
- RT role clarity.
- Setup, troubleshooting, and cleaning/disposal workflow.
- Interdisciplinary training plan.
- Compatibility with airway carts and documentation.
- Escalation path for device or workflow failure.

## Language patterns
- Use: airway support, ventilation, capnography, suction, interdisciplinary workflow, readiness.
- Avoid: physician-only messaging or vague "team adoption."

## Outbound review criteria
- Names RT's operational role.
- Avoids dumping ownership on RT.
- Makes setup and support practical.
- Connects to airway safety and documentation.
