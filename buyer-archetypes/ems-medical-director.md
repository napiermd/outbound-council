# EMS Medical Director

## Evidence status
- Public research + field-note ready.

## Jobs to be done
- Protect clinical quality across variable crews, scenes, and patient physiology.
- Decide whether airway equipment and protocols improve outcomes without creating new risk.
- Defend changes to protocols, education, QA, and medical oversight.

## Buying triggers
- Failed or difficult airway review.
- First-pass success, hypoxia, aspiration, or unrecognized esophageal-intubation concerns.
- New protocol cycle, equipment replacement, training refresh, or QA initiative.
- Peer agency adoption or regional medical-control conversation.

## Objections
- "We already have a protocol and equipment."
- "Will this change behavior or just add another device?"
- "How does this affect BVM/SGA/ETI decision-making?"
- "What data will we review after use?"

## Proof needed
- Clinical rationale by airway scenario.
- Training burden and competency plan.
- QA/QI review workflow.
- Evidence boundaries and escalation language.
- Cost and deployment practicality for field use.

## Language patterns
- Use: protocol fit, medical oversight, airway quality, first-pass performance, hypoxia avoidance, capnography, QA loop.
- Avoid: gadget language, unsupported outcome claims, or implying intubation is always the desired endpoint.

## Outbound review criteria
- Names a clinically credible airway-readiness problem.
- Asks a protocol/QI question, not a purchasing question first.
- Makes the medical director safer for exploring the change.
- Does not overclaim clinical outcomes or AI autonomy.
