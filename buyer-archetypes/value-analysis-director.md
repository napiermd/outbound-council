# Value Analysis Director

## Evidence status
- Public research + field-note ready.

## Jobs to be done
- Decide whether a product is clinically justified, economically defensible, operationally feasible, and risk-reducing.
- Protect the institution from redundant SKUs, weak evidence, supply disruption, and unmanaged adoption.

## Buying triggers
- Clinician request, trial request, adverse-event review, cost-standardization initiative, infection-control issue, or contract cycle.

## Objections
- "What problem does this solve that current products do not?"
- "Who sponsors it clinically and economically?"
- "What is the full cost of adoption?"
- "Will this increase SKU complexity?"

## Proof needed
- Value dossier.
- Comparator and current-state costs.
- Stakeholder map.
- Evidence and claims boundaries.
- Trial plan with success criteria and decision date.
- Supply, training, waste, and policy implications.

## Language patterns
- Use: value dossier, clinical sponsor, economic sponsor, risk reduction, standardization, comparator, pilot criteria.
- Avoid: "cheap," "innovative," or "clinicians love it" without evidence.

## Outbound review criteria
- Includes the comparator and decision criteria.
- Has a clear clinical/economic sponsor path.
- Anticipates operational burden.
- Avoids unsupported savings claims.
