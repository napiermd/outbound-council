# Infection Prevention Lead

## Evidence status
- Public research + field-note ready.

## Jobs to be done
- Reduce contamination and reprocessing risk while keeping clinical workflows practical.
- Ensure device-use claims and policies align with infection-prevention standards.
- Evaluate single-use tradeoffs with evidence and operational realism.

## Buying triggers
- Reprocessing concern, outbreak response, policy review, high-risk airway workflow, or reusable-device audit.
- Clinician request for single-use option.

## Objections
- "What contamination risk is actually being reduced?"
- "What is the disposal and waste impact?"
- "Does this change policy?"
- "Are claims appropriately supported?"

## Proof needed
- Single-use rationale.
- Reprocessing comparator.
- Handling/disposal workflow.
- Policy language and evidence boundaries.
- Training for contamination-prone scenarios.

## Language patterns
- Use: contamination risk, reprocessing, single-use, policy, handling, disposal, infection prevention.
- Avoid: fear-based infection language or unproven infection-rate claims.

## Outbound review criteria
- Names a concrete infection-prevention concern.
- Separates sterility/readiness from outcome claims.
- Addresses disposal and policy.
- Avoids implying all reusable systems are unsafe.
