## Positioning
Ambu positions around single-use endoscopy leadership, integrated airway visualization, broad device access, workflow simplification, and reducing reusable-scope costs/reprocessing burden.

## Claims to track
- Single airway visualization platform.
- Dual-view airway visualization with VL and bronchoscopy tools.
- Single-use efficiency, broad blade portfolio, anti-fog imaging, documentation.
- Reduced costs associated with reusable capital, reprocessing, and repairs.

## Likely buyer appeal
- One trusted supply partner.
- Strong single-use endoscopy credibility.
- Broad inside-hospital portfolio.
- Good fit for anesthesia/OR buyers who want a platform rather than a single device.

## IntuBlade counter-positioning
- Do not compete as "Ambu but smaller" or "cheaper single-use."
- Emphasize deployment-specific airway readiness, EMS/hospital edge use, physician-founder airway workflow, and reviewable training/QI loops.
- In EMS, position around simple field deployment and coverage rather than broad OR visualization platform.

## Evidence gaps / risks
- Ambu has strong portfolio credibility; IntuBlade needs specific proof for its own wedge.
- Avoid claiming lower infection rates or better image quality without evidence.
- Ask whether the buyer needs an integrated platform or a focused disposable VL deployment model.

## Outbound-safe language
- "If your team is already evaluating broad single-use airway platforms, the narrower question may be where video airway coverage is still unavailable or too cumbersome to deploy."
- "IntuBlade may fit the gaps where a full platform is more than the workflow needs."
