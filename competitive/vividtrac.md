## Positioning
VividTrac has been positioned as a low-cost, single-use, USB-based video intubation device with open display options, portability, no battery, and recording potential.

## Claims to track
- Single-use video intubation.
- USB open-system display compatibility.
- Integrated ET tube channel.
- No battery/maintenance.
- EMS portability and recording to ePCR/medical record.

## Likely buyer appeal
- Low-cost disposable video airway concept.
- Open display architecture.
- EMS-specific appeal around portability and recording.
- Channelled device simplicity for some users.

## IntuBlade counter-positioning
- This is the closest conceptual substitute for low-cost disposable video access.
- Differentiate on current availability, support, clinical workflow, blade/tube delivery preference, training program, and evidence package.
- Avoid copying "low-cost USB" positioning unless IntuBlade has a stronger modern deployment story.

## Evidence gaps / risks
- Some source pages are old or distributor-based; verify current commercial availability before using in active sales comparisons.
- Avoid claiming VividTrac is obsolete or unavailable without current confirmation.

## Outbound-safe language
- "Earlier disposable video-intubation devices showed demand for portable video access. IntuBlade's job is to make that model clinically credible, supported, and easy to deploy now."
