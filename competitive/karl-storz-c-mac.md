## Positioning
Karl Storz positions around premium engineering, modular airway systems, C-MAC platform breadth, reusable/single-use options, and airway management across sites of care.

## Claims to track
- First commercially available video laryngoscope.
- Comprehensive airway solutions from ED to ICU.
- Modular system tailored to clinical needs.
- C-MAC S single-use blades and HD monitor ecosystem.
- Premium engineering and hybrid reusable/single-use portfolio.

## Likely buyer appeal
- Premium brand trust.
- Strong anesthesia/OR familiarity.
- Modular platform for hospitals that want one visualization ecosystem.
- Reusable plus single-use flexibility.

## IntuBlade counter-positioning
- Do not fight C-MAC as a premium system head-on.
- Focus on speed of deployment, coverage at the edge, simpler stocking, and situations where a modular premium ecosystem is more than needed.
- For EMS and offsite use, highlight readiness and simplicity over platform depth.

## Evidence gaps / risks
- C-MAC has strong institutional trust and engineering reputation.
- IntuBlade needs clear "why this use case" language and economic justification.

## Outbound-safe language
- "C-MAC may remain the premium platform. IntuBlade may address the coverage gap where teams need a ready disposable video option without building another platform workflow."
