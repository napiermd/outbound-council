# Medtronic McGRATH

## Positioning
McGRATH positions around portable handheld video laryngoscopy, familiar Macintosh technique, routine-to-difficult airway use, durability, battery management, affordability, and large-company support.

## Claims to track
- Designed for OR, ICU, ER, and EMS.
- Combines line-of-sight video with familiar Macintosh technique.
- Disposable blades and portable handle.
- Claims around first-attempt performance, ergonomics, clinician safety, portability, and cost effectiveness.
- MAC+ adds larger/higher-resolution screen and streaming/recording workflow.

## Likely buyer appeal
- Familiar blade technique.
- Handheld portability.
- Medtronic brand and contracting presence.
- Good fit for routine use with disposable blades.

## IntuBlade counter-positioning
- Do not frame against Medtronic on brand scale.
- Focus on IntuBlade's specific deployment economics, fully disposable model if applicable, EMS kit fit, training/review workflow, and physician-founder service layer.
- Ask whether the buyer wants a durable handle/disposable blade system or a single-use disposable device model.

## Evidence gaps / risks
- McGRATH has strong clinical/economic claims and a major incumbent vendor.
- IntuBlade must be precise about lifecycle cost, waste, battery/maintenance, and stocking differences.

## Outbound-safe language
- "If McGRATH is your routine system, the question may be whether every high-risk location and crew has ready video access without handle logistics."
- "IntuBlade can be evaluated as a coverage and deployment model, not only a blade comparison."
