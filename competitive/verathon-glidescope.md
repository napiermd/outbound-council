## Positioning
GlideScope positions as the airway visualization leader with broad single-use/reusable options, proven clinical performance, first-pass success, broad procedure coverage, and a total airway solution.

## Claims to track
- Inventors/market leader in video laryngoscopy.
- Broad single-use and reusable portfolio.
- Improved laryngeal exposure, lower lift force, higher first-pass success in cited studies.
- Total airway solution combining VL and bronchoscopy.

## Likely buyer appeal
- Incumbent credibility and clinical familiarity.
- Broad blade/monitor ecosystem.
- Strong evidence and brand trust.
- Hospital comfort with established vendor support.

## IntuBlade counter-positioning
- Do not attack GlideScope's clinical reputation.
- Position IntuBlade around coverage gaps: disposable access everywhere, simpler deployment, training/QI reviewability, EMS readiness, and low-friction expansion where premium systems are not available at the bedside.
- Ask whether the buyer's issue is "best system in the hospital" or "video airway access wherever an airway happens."

## Evidence gaps / risks
- GlideScope has substantial evidence and portfolio depth; IntuBlade needs proof by use case.
- Avoid broad first-pass superiority claims.
- Be ready to explain why another VL matters when GlideScope is already present.

## Outbound-safe language
- "GlideScope may be the right premium system for many rooms. The gap we care about is the airway that happens where that system is not stocked, ready, or economically practical."
- "This is about expanding video airway coverage, not asking teams to abandon trusted systems."
