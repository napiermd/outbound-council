## Positioning
Reusable capital-equipment workflows position around premium image quality, platform standardization, durable hardware, existing contracts, and centralized cart/monitor ecosystems.

## Claims to track
- Standardized monitor/platform.
- Reusable handles/blades or hybrid portfolio.
- Per-use economics once capital is purchased.
- Existing training and service infrastructure.

## Likely buyer appeal
- Already budgeted and familiar.
- Vendor support and service contracts.
- Strong OR/hospital fit.
- Perceived lower disposable waste.

## IntuBlade counter-positioning
- Do not argue that reusable capital systems are categorically wrong.
- Ask where the capital system fails to cover low-volume, offsite, EMS, backup, or contamination-prone workflows.
- Position IntuBlade as additive coverage, trial wedge, or lower-friction deployment where capital carts are not practical.

## Evidence gaps / risks
- Economic comparison depends heavily on utilization, reprocessing, repair, stocking, and waste assumptions.
- Need buyer-specific budget-impact model before making savings claims.

## Outbound-safe language
- "Capital systems can make sense in high-utilization locations. The question is where video airway access still depends on finding, cleaning, charging, or moving the right equipment."
