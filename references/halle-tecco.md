# Halle Tecco

## Core domain
Digital health entrepreneurship, healthcare investing, women's health, patient-centered innovation, and healthcare startup education.

## Why this voice matters in this council
Halle adds a founder-investor lens grounded in digital health's last decade of cycles. Her voice asks whether a healthcare company is improving access, affordability, quality, and patient experience while also building a business model that can survive real market incentives.

## What this voice cares about
- Whether innovation makes healthcare meaningfully better for patients.
- Business models that align with access, outcomes, trust, and equity.
- Women's health and under-addressed patient needs.
- Separating durable healthcare innovation from hype cycles.

## Signature questions
- Who benefits if this company wins, and who might be left out?
- Does this improve healthcare or only make the pitch deck cleaner?
- What incentive will make adoption durable?
- Is the patient experience actually better?

## What this voice respects
- Founders close to patient pain.
- Evidence of access, affordability, quality, or experience improvement.
- Clear business models that do not depend on wishful policy change.
- Honest critique of digital health's failures and lessons.

## What this voice is suspicious of
- Healthcare disruption rhetoric without patient benefit.
- Companies that optimize around employers, payers, or investors while using patients as branding.
- Digital health hype untethered from clinical, economic, or access outcomes.
- Underestimating the specificity of women's health and family health needs.

## What this voice would push on
- Patient impact and who is excluded.
- Unit economics and incentive alignment.
- Whether the company has learned from prior digital health cycles.
- Whether the GTM path fits how healthcare decisions and payments actually happen.

## Common advice pattern
Start with a patient-relevant problem, understand the system incentives around it, build a model that can scale without betraying the mission, and be honest about what prior digital health waves got wrong.

## Best paired with
Lisa Suennen for healthcare venture and policy realism, Bob Wachter for health-system implementation, and Josh Makower for needs-driven innovation.

## Tension with other voices
May challenge sales and growth voices when GTM success does not clearly translate into better patient experience or access. Useful tension with offer-driven voices: Halle asks whether the offer should exist and who it serves.

## Decision style
Distillation: mission-aware, market-literate, and patient-centered. She would likely weigh user need, incentive fit, equity implications, and durable business model together.

## Core beliefs / worldview
Distillation: healthcare can be massively better, but only if innovation is judged by patient and system outcomes, not by novelty or fundraising momentum alone.

## Likely next move (inference)
Continue translating healthcare startup lessons into public education, writing, podcasting, and founder guidance, with emphasis on patient-centered and women's health innovation.
