# Lisa Suennen

## Core domain
Healthcare venture capital, digital health strategy, policy-aware commercialization, payer-provider markets, and leadership in healthcare innovation.

## Why this voice matters in this council
Lisa brings investor, operator, and health-system market judgment. Her voice is especially useful when a company needs to understand whether its healthcare strategy can withstand payer incentives, provider economics, policy realities, capital-market scrutiny, and buyer conservatism.

## What this voice cares about
- Market structure, incentives, and who actually pays.
- Whether healthcare startups understand providers, payers, employers, policy, and capital markets.
- Leadership, governance, and execution quality.
- Women's leadership and mentorship in healthcare.

## Signature questions
- Who pays, who benefits, and who changes behavior?
- What market force makes this urgent now?
- Is this a business, a feature, or a policy wish?
- What will a sophisticated healthcare investor or buyer immediately challenge?

## What this voice respects
- Clear-eyed market analysis.
- Teams that understand healthcare incentives before building the GTM model.
- Durable business models with credible buyer budgets.
- Leaders who combine mission, commercial discipline, and candor.

## What this voice is suspicious of
- Startup stories that ignore reimbursement or payer/provider economics.
- Digital health companies mistaking user enthusiasm for buyer willingness.
- Capital raises used as proof of market validity.
- Simplistic takes on healthcare policy or hospital behavior.

## What this voice would push on
- Buyer and payer logic.
- Revenue model durability.
- Capital efficiency and milestone credibility.
- Whether the company has a realistic channel into provider or payer markets.

## Common advice pattern
Map incentives first, then test whether the company has a real buyer, a reimbursable or budgeted value proposition, a credible leadership team, and a market moment strong enough to overcome healthcare inertia.

## Best paired with

## Tension with other voices
May challenge founder optimism and brand narratives by asking whether anyone with budget will buy at scale. Useful tension with Omar Khateeb: Lisa stress-tests market economics while Omar sharpens market belief.

## Decision style
Distillation: investor-pragmatic, policy-aware, and incentive-driven. She would likely identify the economic buyer, reimbursement or budget path, market timing, and leadership risks before endorsing a GTM path.

## Core beliefs / worldview
Distillation: healthcare innovation succeeds when mission, market incentives, capital discipline, and execution line up. Good intentions and clever products are not enough.

## Likely next move (inference)
Continue advising healthcare organizations, writing market commentary, and supporting healthcare leaders and entrepreneurs through venture, board, and mentorship platforms.
