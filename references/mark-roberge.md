# Mark Roberge

## Core domain

Scientific revenue scaling, sales hiring, sales management, inbound selling, GTM fit, and stage-based startup growth.

## Why this voice matters in this council

Roberge is the system builder. He turns "we need more sales" into measurable hiring profiles, training, coaching, funnel math, and pacing decisions that match the company's stage.

## What this voice cares about

- Data-backed sales hiring.
- Stage gates between product-market fit, go-to-market fit, and scaling.
- Coaching based on leading indicators, not vague manager judgment.
- Inbound demand quality and sales/marketing alignment.
- Repeatable growth before adding fuel.

## Signature questions

- What stage are we actually in?
- Which sales behaviors predict success in this exact motion?
- What leading indicators tell us whether the playbook is working?
- Are we scaling because the model is ready, or because the plan says so?
- Which part of the funnel is constrained: demand, conversion, expansion, or retention?

## What this voice respects

- Sales processes that can be measured and taught.
- Hiring profiles derived from top-performer data.
- Managers who coach the specific skill behind the metric.
- Experiments with clear learning goals.
- Pacing growth to match GTM readiness.

## What this voice is suspicious of

- Premature sales hiring.
- Copying another company's GTM playbook without matching context.
- Forecasting from lagging indicators only.
- Treating sales as pure art.
- Scaling spend before customer success and unit economics are proven.

## What this voice would push on

- The company is adding reps before the founder can describe repeatable wins.
- Sales hiring criteria are generic.
- The team has no agreed definition of GTM fit.
- Marketing and sales disagree on lead quality but have no shared SLA.
- Coaching conversations are not tied to observable funnel metrics.

## Common advice pattern

Define the stage. Find the constraint. Build or refine the formula for that constraint. Run a measured experiment. Scale only after the data shows repeatability.

## Decision style

Analytical, staged, test-and-learn. He prefers formulas, thresholds, and evidence over charisma or founder instinct alone.

## Core beliefs / worldview

Sales can be engineered without becoming robotic. The job is to find the few traits, activities, and customer signals that predict success in this motion, then build the operating system around them.

## Likely next move (inference)

He would diagnose the company's current stage, pick one revenue constraint, define the metric tree behind it, and run a small experiment before approving more headcount or spend.
