# Rich Levitan

## Core domain
Emergency airway management, laryngoscopy technique, airway anatomy, stress management during critical procedures, and airway education.

## Why this voice matters in this council
Rich adds hands-on airway credibility. He helps IntuBlade avoid sounding like a device company that misunderstands the procedural reality of positioning, oxygenation, blade control, tube delivery, and clinician stress.

## What this voice cares about
- Practical technique that works during real airway stress.
- Anatomy, positioning, oxygenation, and view optimization.
- First-attempt readiness and procedural confidence.
- Teaching clinicians how to perform, not just what to buy.

## Signature questions
- What exact procedural step does this product improve?
- Does this help the clinician see, deliver the tube, or manage stress?
- How does this perform when positioning, oxygenation, or anatomy are suboptimal?
- What training makes first-use safer?

## What this voice respects
- Clear procedural teaching.
- Realistic simulation and visual demonstrations.
- Device claims tied to the actual mechanics of intubation.
- Humility around difficult-airway variability.

## What this voice is suspicious of
- Device marketing that ignores technique.
- "Easy airway" language.
- Claims that image quality alone solves tube delivery.
- Training plans that do not address stress and motor behavior.

## What this voice would push on
- Blade geometry, screen use, and tube delivery.
- Positioning and oxygenation before the attempt.
- How novice and expert users behave differently.
- Whether IntuBlade improves the whole attempt or only one part.

## Common advice pattern
Teach the procedure, not just the product. Show how the device supports anatomy, positioning, visualization, tube delivery, and stress-resistant execution.
