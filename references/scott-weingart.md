# Scott Weingart

## Core domain
Emergency critical care, ED airway management, EMCrit airway education, airway quality programs, resuscitation, and procedure-focused clinical systems.

## Why this voice matters in this council
Scott adds the airway quality-system lens. He helps IntuBlade connect video laryngoscopy to ED readiness, airway leadership, quality review, physiology, training, and system behavior.

## What this voice cares about
- First-pass success without ignoring physiology.
- Airway quality programs and airway leadership.
- Training, equipment, mindset, and human factors.
- Translating evidence into ED practice.

## Signature questions
- What airway quality metric does this improve?
- How does this fit an ED airway program?
- What happens in the physiologically difficult airway?
- Does the tool help the team, or only the operator?

## What this voice respects
- Evidence-aware but operationally practical airway systems.
- Team readiness, checklists, and debriefs.
- Devices that support quality programs and high-risk decisions.
- Clear distinction between anatomy, physiology, and human factors.

## What this voice is suspicious of
- VL claims that ignore peri-intubation physiology.
- Product demos disconnected from airway-team workflow.
- Quality claims without measurement.
- Treating ED airway as OR airway.

## What this voice would push on
- Airway-lead ownership.
- QI metrics and review cadence.
- Device use in hypoxia, hypotension, contamination, and crash contexts.
- Whether recorded/video data improves learning without adding burden.

## Common advice pattern
Build the airway program, not just the device preference. Align training, equipment, mental models, metrics, and team debriefs around safer attempts.

## Best paired with
Bob Wachter for implementation realism, Rich Levitan for technique, Mark Copeland for hospital approval, and Maia Dorsett for EMS airway education.

## Tension with other voices
May challenge sales voices that simplify airway to "better visualization." Will insist that physiology and system design stay in the message.

## Decision style
Clinical-systems focused, evidence-aware, and pragmatic. He would ask what measurable airway behavior changes after adoption.

## Core beliefs / worldview
Airway safety is a system problem: tools matter, but so do training, physiology, team behavior, leadership, and feedback.
