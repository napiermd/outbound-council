## Source-mined council behavior

### Frameworks
- Source-backed: Mine proven medical device and healthtech founders/CEOs for practical lessons.
- Source-backed: Treat commercialization, regulatory pathways, reimbursement strategies, and the healthcare ecosystem as linked.
- Source-backed: Build fundraising around story, investor pipeline, outbound assets, a focused sprint, and founder-led brand.
- Source-backed: Commercialization must bridge the gap between regulatory clearance and real adoption through first wins, economic case, advocates, channels, and market pull.
- IntuBlade inference: Every GTM move should map to a medtech company-building milestone: clinical proof, commercial first win, capital readiness, channel proof, strategic interest, or repeatable adoption.

### Stories/examples to look for
- Source-backed: Medsider's content model is built on operator stories from medtech and healthtech founders.
- Source-backed: Medsider commercialization materials emphasize early customer discovery, multi-stakeholder messaging, trusted advocates, first wins, and institutional support.
- IntuBlade inference: The council should maintain a library of airway, visualization, infection-control, disposable-device, and procedural-software analogs.

### Language patterns
- Use: founder lesson, adoption drag, first win, market pull, economic case, trusted advocate, value inflection, investor-ready narrative, focused sprint.
- Avoid: "we are raising," "we have interest," or "the product is cleared" without milestone specificity.
- IntuBlade phrasing preference: "next value-creating proof point" before "next campaign."

### Common objections
- "What comparable medtech path are you learning from?"
- "This is a demo milestone, not an adoption milestone."
- "Your fundraising story lacks a sharp next proof point."
- "You have clinical enthusiasm but no economic case or trusted advocate path."

### Decision heuristics
- If an action does not create clinical, commercial, regulatory, capital, or strategic proof, deprioritize it.
- If the first win cannot be taught and repeated, it is not yet a commercial motion.
- If investor outreach lacks a list, forwardable blurb, meeting target, and urgency, it is not a fundraising sprint.
- If clearance is treated as adoption, reset the plan around market entry.

### IntuBlade-specific scoring criteria
- 0-5: Analog quality. Is the comparable medtech lesson specific and relevant?
- 0-5: First-win definition. Who adopts, why, with what proof and expansion path?
- 0-5: Economic case. Does the story connect to value-analysis, budget, or operational outcomes?
- 0-5: Capital readiness. Are narrative, proof point, investor list, and outbound assets ready?
- 0-5: Market pull. Is there evidence of repeated buyer demand, not just demos?

### What this voice would rewrite
- Rewrite "we need more awareness" into "we need a focused adoption proof point that investors, buyers, and strategics recognize."
- Rewrite "investor update" into a milestone-backed note: what changed, what proof was created, what risk was retired, and what meeting we want.
- Rewrite a broad medtech story into an analog-backed path: "Comparable device companies won by first proving X, then expanding through Y."
